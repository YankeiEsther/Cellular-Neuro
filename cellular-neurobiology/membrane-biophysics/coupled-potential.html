Coupled Potential

Note

  1. constant flux of glucose and oxygen into cells
  2. Oxidation of glucose is coupled to the generation of ion gradient
  3. Ion gradient is coupled to the generation of ATP/ADP gradient
  4. ATP/ADP gradient in cytoplasm is coupled to the generation of ion gradients across the cell membrane
  5. The cell membrane sodium gradient coupled to the transport of different solutes and generation of electric potential gradient
Stoichiometry of pumps and transporters
  • The energy provided by the energetically favorable process has to match or exceed the potential gradient of the energetically unfavorable process
    • in order for the pump or transporter to function
  • potential gradient of solute can differ between different compartments or cells
    • identical pumps or transporters can have different stoichiometries

Ca-ATPase

  • PMCA moves one calcium ion for expenditure of one ATP molecule
  • SERCA moves two calcium ion for expenditure of one ATP molecule
  • For PMCA, Vm = -0.07 V
  • For SERCA, Vm = 0
    • There is little to no electric potential difference across ER membrane
      • electrochemical potential difference for calcium across ER membrane is significantly lower
      • ATP hydrolysis provides sufficient energy to transport 2 Ca ions

Transporters

  • transporters are reversible
    • direction of transport depends only on the balance of electrochemical and chemical potentials of the transported ions and solutes
Na/Glucose Transporter

  • belongs to SLC5 family
    • SGLT1: 2 Na+ ions are coupled to the transport of each glucose molecule
    • SGLT2: 1 Na+ ions is coupled to the transport of each glucose molecule

    • SGLT1 and SGLT2 are expressed in kidney
      • SGLT1 can transport glucose up a much steeper concentration gradient (up to ~16,400 fold) before reversing
        • located in late proximal tubule, where concentration of glucose ([glucose]out) is much lower (steeper concentration gradient)
      • SGLT2 can transport glucose against a concentration gradient up to 128 fold before reversing
        • located in early proximal tubule, where glucose concentration is high

  • Sodium as the transported solute; calcium as the coupled ion

  • electrochemical potential difference

      • transport of calcium out of the cell become increasingly favored
    • Depolarization of membrane without an increase in internal calcium concentration
      • Calcium ions will flow into the cell (reaction reverse)
Action potential and Ca concentration
  • internal calcium concentration and membrane potential change significantly during an action potential

    Action potential → Vm ↑ → ↑ → Ca influx
  • although Ca flux through channels usually contribute much more to increase in Ca concentration
    - reversal of Na/Ca exchanger contribute to influx of calcium ions
    - exchanger reverse during the upstroke of the action potential
