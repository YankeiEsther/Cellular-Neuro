Enthalpy

Definition
  • U = the sum of the system's internal energy
  • p = pressure
  • V = volume of the system
  • = exothermic = system giving off energy as heat to the surrounding
    • forming a bond is exothermic
  • = endothermic = system gaining heat from the surrounding
    • breaking a bond is endothermic
  • enthalpy of a products and reactants of a reaction equals to their total bond energy
    • Exothermic reaction () -> when breaking a reactant with weak bond and forming a product with strong bond
    • Endothermic reaction () → when breaking a reactant with strong bond and forming a product with weak bond

Negative Gibbs free energy is favorable

  • A reaction or process will only go forward spontaneously if the change in Gibbs free energy is negative
  • Reaction doesn't have to be drive by a positive entropy
    • Entropy can decrease if the change in enthalpy is negative enough to offset the decrease in entropy

Diffusion

Diffusion of Solute - 'last mile' problem
  • Movement of solute throughout the body is achieved by either bulk flow or diffusion
    • bulk flow can move solutes over large distances within the body
    • Diffusion can only move solutes over relatively short distances with any efficiency
  • Last mile problem
    • it is relatively inexpensive to build the infrastructure (vascular system) to bring the solute close to its final destination
      • but very expensive to deliver directly to every cell
    • the last mile in delivering solute to cell is largely achieved by diffusion
      • efficient over short distance

Diffusion and equilibrium

  • diffusion of a small fraction of solute molecules can be very fast
    • but it can be very slow to reach equilibrium
  • Example: Calcium signaling in presynaptic nerve terminal
    • During action potential
      • there is a brief increase in membrane permeability to calcium ions
        • allows a relatively small number of calcium enter the cell rapidly → trigger neurotransmitter release

Diffusion and entropy

  • diffusion is an increase in entropy
  • As the entropy of the system increases the ability to perform work decrease
  • Clausius - entropy is that part of the energy which cannot be converted into work
    • there is a constant flux of energy into biological system
      • creating gradients of ions and solutes across the cell membrane
        • locally decrease entropy in the cell

Multiplicity

Why is there an equal number of solute molecules on both sides of the membrane at equilibrium
  • Thermodynamic explanation at equilibrium is statistical
  • when there are a large number of solute molecules, it is vastly more likely that there will be a even distribution of solute molecules between to equal sized compartments
    • Explained by the binomial equation

  • N = number of solute molecules in a given compartment
  • M = the total number of solute molecules
  • If N = 2 and M = 4
    • W = 6, meaning that there will be 6 different ways to equally distribute the 4 solute
    • vs N=4 and M=4 → W = 1, there only one way to distribute all molecules in one compartment
  • Each arrangement are equally likely
    - meaning that it is 6 times more likely to equally distribute than clustered all in 1 compartment
  • As M increased (molecule increase)
    • it become very unlikely that all the solute will cluster in one compartment
    • it become more likely that the solute will equally distributed between two compartment

Boltzmann equation

  • S = entropy; k = Boltzmann constant
  • describe the relationship between multiplicity and entropy
    • Entropy is a logarithmic transformation of multiplicity
  • This is a statistical way to define entropy
    • The system tends to evolve towards the maximum multiplicity, because this is the highest probability state
  • As the second law state that entropy tends to increase in an isolated system
    • it is equivalent to saying that *the system will tend towards the most probable state, the state with the **highest multiplicity***
